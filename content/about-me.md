**Justin Wheeler** is an experienced Open Source enthusiast with a passion for building vibrant, sustainable communities.
He is currently the [Fedora Community Architect](https://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/council/fca/) (FCA) at [Red Hat](https://www.redhat.com/), where he supports and enables the [Fedora](/categories/fedora/) contributor community.

With over a decade of experience in Open Source, Justin has a proven track record of fostering collaboration and innovation.
He's worked on various projects, from contributing to the [CHAOSS community](https://chaoss.community/) to leading open source empowerment programs at [UNICEF](/categories/unicef/).
At UNICEF, he supported the [UNICEF Venture Fund](https://www.unicefventurefund.org/) and other Open Source activities within the Office of Innovation.
Together with a team of mentors, Justin mentored twenty-three start-up companies of diverse start-up companies from nineteen countries and five continents.
Of these companies, fourteen achieved global recognition as [Digital Public Goods](https://digitalpublicgoods.net/digital-public-goods/) and three were acquired in multi-million dollar acquisitions.

As a [Fedora](/categories/fedora/) community member since 2015, Justin has played a pivotal role in shaping its direction.
He serves as a team leader, advocating for [diversity and inclusion](https://docs.fedoraproject.org/en-US/dei/) and representing Fedora at international events.
Outside of Fedora, Justin is a frequent speaker at conferences like [OS Community Africa Festival](https://oscafest23.sched.com/event/1NCdz/building-an-operating-system-choose-your-own-adventure-in-open-source-contribution?iframe=no), CHAOSScon ([Africa](https://chaoss.community/chaosscon-2023-africa/) & [Europe](/blog/2020/02/chaosscon-eu-2020-play-by-play/) editions), [FOSDEM](/blog/2020/04/fosdem-2020-pt-2-can-free-software-include-ethical-ai-systems/), [DevConf CZ](https://devconf.info/cz), [All Things Open](https://allthingsopen.org/talk/what-open-source-and-j-k-rowling-have-in-common/), [OSCAL](https://oscal.openlabs.cc/), and others.
He's also a current or past contributor of other Open Source projects, like [SpigotMC](https://www.spigotmc.org/wiki/about-spigot/), [Opensource.com](https://opensource.com/users/jflory), [MusicBrainz](https://musicbrainz.org/doc/About), and Wikipedia.
